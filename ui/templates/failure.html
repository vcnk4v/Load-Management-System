FAILURE ANALYSIS

Sensor Failure

  • Voltage and Current Sensors

    Voltage and Current sensors require proper calibration to provide accurate readings. If the sensors are not calibrated correctly, they may produce incorrect voltage measurements, leading to inaccurate decisions regarding power source switching.

  • LDR (Light Dependant Resistor)

    LDR may degrade over time due to wear and tear. This can lead to inaccurate light measurements, affecting the system's ability to accurately assess solar irradiation levels

Relay Failure

Relays can suffer wear and tear , which can cause a variety of issues like contact degradation . Relay Coil failure is also another potential error. The coil within a relay is responsible for the electromagnetic switching mechanism. When the coil fails, the relay may become unable to switch between power sources effectively, leading to improper power distribution and potential imbalances in load prioritization.

Communication Errors

  • Latency

    Delays in network communication can disrupt the timely transmission of data and control signals, leading to inaccurate load management decisions and potentially causing imbalances in power distribution among different loads..

  • Transmission Malfunction

    Without appropriate error handling, malfunctions in data transmission can lead to inaccuracies in the readings of voltage sensors. These inaccuracies can then propagate through the load management system, causing errors in load prioritization and potentially leading to improper power distribution.

Environmental Factors

  • Overheating

    Exposure to sunlight can cause overheating in the sensors . This can degrade the components , giving rise to inaccurate values.

  • Exposure to Dust

    Accumulation of dust on the surface of the solar panel reduces the effciency of the power conversion , reducing overall effciency of the system.

  • Humidity

    Humidity can cause corrosion leading to component degradation which can cause the sensors in the system to malfunction and can potentially be hazardous .
